Islamic Science Contributions

Optics

Islamic scientists made many contributions to the area of eyesight/optics such as eyeglass lenses themselves! From 810-887, Abbas ibn Firnas was the first person to develop lenses for magnification and the improvement of vision.

Medicine

Islamic scientists made a lot of impacts on how our medical system works to this day, from better knowledge of the body in general to mental institutions. Islamic hospitals were the first to accept people with mental illnesses. Islamic doctors used natural music in an effort to treat these patients. Because of this, an orchestra platform was constructed at an Allepo hospital, and every hospital had a fountain at the center so that the patients could be "cured" by listening to the flowing water

Mathematics

Islamic empires also had a big impact on the mathematics of today. Jamshid al-Kashi is credited with several modern theorems of trigonometry such as the law of cosines, or Al-Kashi's Theorem. He also is credited as being the inventor of decimal fractions, as well as an efficient method to calculate roots before calculators came into existence.
